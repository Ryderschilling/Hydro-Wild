What makes a kids hydration drink actually good

Before you buy, these are the criteria pediatric-minded parents weigh. A genuinely healthy kids hydration drink should check every box:

  • Zero (or very low) added sugar — most kids sports drinks carry more sugar than a soda.
  • No artificial dyes or colors — synthetic dyes are exactly what parents are trying to avoid.
  • Real electrolytes — potassium and magnesium — the minerals that drive hydration, not just sodium.
  • Added vitamins — turns a hydration drink into daily nutritional support.
  • No caffeine — a hydration drink for kids should never be an energy drink.
  • A clean sweetener — stevia over sucralose, aspartame, or sugar alcohols.
  • Made in a regulated facility and third-party tested — proof of purity and potency.
  • A flavor kids will actually finish — the healthiest drink is the one they drink.
